Welcome to Brooklyn College Helpline

Personal counseling is available to Brooklyn College undergraduate and graduate students.

About Us

A staff of psychologists, counselors, and social workers are available to assist you with personal issues or problems you may experience during your academic years. Emergency consultations and referrals to outside services are also provided. Workshops are offered on stress management, time management, test anxiety, etc.

Our Services

  • Individual Therapy
  • Group Therapy
  • Theme-centered workshops
  • Housing Referral
  • Drug and Alcohol Counseling
  • Video showings and discussion groups

Expectations for Therapy

  • Initial Sessions: Includes an assessment, goal setting, and establishing rapport.
  • Regular Sessions: Typically weekly or biweekly, focusing on open communication and active participation.
  • Confidentiality: Ensures privacy, with exceptions for risks of harm.
  • Therapeutic Techniques: Various approaches are used based on your needs, such as CBT or psychodynamic therapy.
  • Progress and Adjustments: Regular reviews and adjustments to the treatment plan, dealing with setbacks as part of the process.
  • Outcomes: Improved coping skills, increased self-awareness, behavioral changes, and emotional relief.
  • Ending Therapy: Therapy ends when goals are achieved or by mutual decision, with possible follow-up sessions.
